Afrika : Love letters

Africa

This self-initiated project is based on celebrating Afrikan countries independence from colonial rule through an exploration of lettering and letter forms. This was initiated by the disturbance of the xenophobic attacks happening in South Africa towards other Afrikan nationals. In educating myself further about each Afrikan country, I started critiquing the state they are in, by means of ancient cultural symbols, art, sculptures or patterns. I also attempt to explore the similarities we have with one another. By doing so I am able to realise that it's more about learning from the past, and from other cultures so in order to preserve who we are and also celebrate one another. The projects consists of more than 40 artworks.

Morocco 64 – Inspired by colourful Moroccan mosaic patterns called Zellij, which is a craft that dates back to at least the 700s. This craft can be found in many architectural structures in Morocco; like mosques, gates and opulent palaces.

Gabon 60 - Inspired by the Bakota reliquary guardian figures (mbulu ngulu), traditionally the Bakota believe in the ancestors powers of the afterlife.

Chad 60 - Inspired by the ancient Wodaabe face tribal scar tattoos which symbolise beauty.

Burkina Faso 60- Inspired by the sun spirit mask of the Bwa people of Burkina Faso, which is used to celebrate the farming season.

Zambia 56 - Inspired by the African fish eagle, the national bird of Zambia, the design on 6 is inspired by the waving patterns on the Makenge baskets.

Eritrea 29 - Inspired by beautiful hand-made patterns called Tibeb on a Habesha dress. A Habesha dress is a traditional attire won by Habesha women. Throughout the history of Eritrea, women have played an active role.
